Observability and Incident Response

Unified Observability Platform & MTTD/MTTR Improvement

Problem: Distributed Linux workloads across 40+ production servers ran with fragmented monitoring stacks: Grafana dashboards in silos, Splunk logs disconnected from APM data, Dynatrace APM isolated from infrastructure metrics. Incident detection took 15+ minutes because SREs had to manually correlate alerts across 4 different systems. Root cause analysis required manual log grepping and cross-platform detective work, extending incident resolution from 45 minutes to 2+ hours. 30% of alerts were false positives, causing alert fatigue and slower human response.

Your Action: Architected unified observability platform consolidating Grafana, Splunk, Dynatrace, and Azure Monitor into single-pane-of-glass dashboards. Built service topology mapping with end-to-end request tracing. Implemented Fluentd collectors on all 40+ nodes for centralized log ingestion. Deployed alert correlation engine with intelligent rule grouping (Python + AlertManager) to reduce noise. Created runbooks linked directly to Grafana dashboards with one-click incident context.

Tools & Technologies: Grafana (dashboard architecture), Splunk (centralized logs), Dynatrace (APM), Azure Monitor, Fluentd (log collection), Prometheus (metrics), AlertManager (correlation), Python (correlation logic).

Business Results: Mean Time to Detect (MTTD) reduced from 15 minutes to 11 minutes (25% faster). Mean Time to Recovery (MTTR) improved from 45 minutes to 37 minutes (18% faster). Alert volume reduced by 35% with 90% true positives. Sustained 99.9% SLA uptime for 6+ consecutive months. Reduced on-call escalations by 40%, improving team morale and reducing context switching. On-call burnout metrics improved 35%.

Automation and Toil Reduction

Runbook Automation & Alert Correlation System

Problem: On-call SREs received 200+ alerts daily with 70% false positive rate. Each false alert required 15 minutes of manual triage to dismiss or escalate. Manual runbooks for incident response were stored in Confluence (outdated and inconsistent), causing response times of 2+ hours for routine issues. Incident recovery process was inconsistent: some issues resolved in 30 minutes by experienced engineers, others took 4+ hours. Knowledge silos meant junior engineers slow to respond, and no learning across incidents due to lack of automation. Estimated 8+ FTE hours daily wasted on alert triage alone.

Your Action: Built Python-based alert correlation engine with intelligent grouping rules to merge related alerts and suppress known false positives. Developed 12+ standardized Bash runbooks covering common incident scenarios (high CPU, memory leaks, failed deployments, disk full, hanging services). Integrated runbooks with PagerDuty for auto-escalation and context passing. Implemented auto-remediation scripts that attempt self-healing for low-risk scenarios (service restart, log rotation, cache clear) with human-in-loop approval for production actions. Created incident response playbook linking alerts → runbooks → auto-remediation with fallback to manual response.

Tools & Technologies: Python (correlation engine), Bash (runbooks and remediation scripts), PagerDuty (incident tracking), Splunk (log context), Git (runbook version control), Ansible (for safe automation actions).

Business Results: Alert volume reduced 35% (from 200+ to 130 daily alerts). False positive rate dropped from 70% to 12%. Average incident response time improved 67%: from 120 minutes to 40 minutes. First-time resolution rate improved 45% (incidents resolved without escalation). Saved 8+ FTE hours per week in toil reduction. Enabled junior engineers to resolve 60% of incidents without senior escalation.
